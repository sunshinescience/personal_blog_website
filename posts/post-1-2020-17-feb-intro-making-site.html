Commencement of a blog site

Feb 17, 2020

Image used under license from Freestock.com

I've had a blog on Github pages since early 2019. There, I wrote about things I had discovered while working on Python projects. And I also included some tutorials (see previous posts here).

This new site is a showcase of my most recent works utilizing HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. It also serves as a place to share things I learn while looking into the tech sector.

The pages on this site were structured with HTML5 and designed with CSS on a Macbook Pro over the course of a Udacity front end developer nanodegree program. I used Adobe Illustrator to design my logo at the top of the site. I used Photoshop to change some of the images.

I spent some time looking at other sites for designs I liked. The design of this site was inspired by what I saw on the sites getbootstrap.com, simonpan.com, and hochburg.design.

I used color schemes I found with the color tool from material.io. Some web fonts considered to be safe across browsers can be found here. For this site I used a variety of fonts, some of which include: Georgia, Cambria, serif, "Times New Roman", Times, Roboto, Helvetica, Arial, and sans-serif.

In my next post, I'll discuss how I learned to keep the footer at the bottom of the web page.
